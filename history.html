The club was founded in September 1992 on the site of the former Kronen Brewery on Biedermannstraße in Leipzig-Connewitz. The idea came from a group of friends. This is also where the name is said to come from — “distillery” being the English term for a place where spirits are produced — even though no hard liquor was ever distilled at the brewery.

The original location was later replaced by a new venue in Südvorstadt. From then on, the club was located in a building on a former Deutsche Bahn site at Kurt-Eisner-Straße 108a.

After many years at Kurt-Eisner-Straße, Distillery moved to its current location at the Alte Messe Leipzig. Since 2025, the club has been based in Hall 7. On August 30, 2025, after almost two years of planning and construction, Distillery reopened its doors in the northern head building of Hall 7.

The story of Distillery’s beginnings was documented in the 2013 film “Willkommen zu Hause – 20 Jahre Distillery”. In 2025, the club was also featured in the special exhibition “Zwischen Aufbruch und Abwicklung. Die 90er in Leipzig” at the Stadtgeschichtliches Museum Leipzig.
